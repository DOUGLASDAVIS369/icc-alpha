In the field

Outreach activities related to the Lubanga trial are conducted mostly in Ituri, the region in eastern DRC where the alleged crimes were committed. Through countless activities with children, youth groups, women, ethnic group leaders and NGO representatives, and by using radio programmes to reach those in even the most remote places, the Court engages in two-way dialogue with the communities most affected by the crimes addressed in this case, relaying updates as the case progressed as well as answering questions and addressing concerns raised by these communities. In particular, Outreach staff carried the message from the Court that conscripting child soldiers is a crime. This two-way dialogue continues as the case is at the appeals stage.
